**ISO/IEC 42001:2023** is the first **AI management-system (AIMS)** standard. It is a
**voluntary, certifiable standard** — **not legislation and not binding on anyone**. It
sits in the ISO harmonized management-system family alongside ISO/IEC 27001 (information
security) and ISO 9001 (quality): an organization can build an AIMS against it and, if it
chooses, have that system **certified by a third party**. Certification attests to the
*management system*, not to any individual AI model's safety.

Because it is a management-system standard, its requirements are organizational and
process-shaped — policy, risk assessment, lifecycle controls, documentation, performance
evaluation, continual improvement — rather than prescriptions about a specific model. On
the atlas that lands on the operational concepts: **evaluation** (the performance-measure
half of "monitoring and measurement"), **verification** (the lifecycle checks),
**observability** (operational monitoring of a deployed system), and **failure modes**
(the AI-specific risks a risk assessment has to name). Adopting it is a real cost — an
audited process to maintain — so whether it is worth certifying depends on the
organization's obligations, customers, and risk appetite.

> Not legal advice, and not a compliance obligation. ISO/IEC 42001 is voluntary; this page
> generalizes its structure and is not a substitute for the standard's own text.
