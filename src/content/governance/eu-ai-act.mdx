The **EU AI Act** (Regulation (EU) 2024/1689) is the first comprehensive AI regulation.
It is **risk-based**: obligations attach according to how a system is classified —
prohibited practices, high-risk systems, limited-risk (transparency) systems, and
minimal-risk — plus a separate regime for **general-purpose AI (GPAI) models**. It binds
**providers** (who develop or place systems on the market) and **deployers** (who use them
professionally), and it reaches any system whose output is used in the EU.

The mappings on this page are **potential relevance**, not determinations: whether a
specific obligation applies depends on the concrete system, its risk classification, the
actor's role, and the jurisdiction. The atlas shows *which technical mechanism a rule
reaches* — the accuracy and robustness duty (Art. 15) is where **evaluation** and
**failure modes** become compliance evidence, and the GPAI training-documentation duty is
where **tokens** (training scale) and **what a language model is** (training vs inference)
surface.
