The **GDPR** (Regulation (EU) 2016/679) governs the **processing of personal data** —
and an LLM system processes personal data in more places than teams expect: the prompt
and conversation history, retrieved documents, the **embeddings** derived from them
(derived personal data is still personal data), the **vector store** that indexes them,
and the request/response logs.

The mappings here are **potential relevance**, not determinations — applicability depends
on whether personal data is actually present, the purpose, the role (controller vs
processor), and the jurisdiction. The atlas shows *where* the duty lands: minimization
and lawful basis reach **context windows** and **RAG** (what you retrieve and inject),
while access and erasure rights reach **embeddings** and **vector search** (you must be
able to find and delete an individual's data in an index, not just in the source file).
