**DORA** — the **Digital Operational Resilience Act**, Regulation (EU) 2022/2554 — is a
**binding EU regulation** that has applied since **17 January 2025**. It sets uniform
requirements for the **operational resilience of the ICT** that financial entities depend
on, built on five pillars: ICT risk management, incident management and reporting,
resilience testing, ICT third-party risk, and information sharing. It also brings
**critical ICT third-party providers** under a direct EU oversight framework.

The mappings on this page are **potential relevance**, not determinations. Whether — and
how — DORA applies depends on the entity type, its ICT arrangements, the specific use
case, and the jurisdiction; DORA governs the *operational resilience* of ICT, not AI as
such. Where an AI system is part of a financial entity's ICT, it inherits that ICT's
obligations. The atlas shows *which technical mechanism a pillar reaches*:
**observability** is where incident detection and reconstruction live, **evaluation** and
**failure modes** are where resilience testing becomes evidence, **verification** is the
in-line checking that keeps a system within tolerance, and **multi-agent systems** are
where composed, third-party-dependent ICT raises the concentration and resilience
questions the third-party pillar is about.
