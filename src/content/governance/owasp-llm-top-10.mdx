The **OWASP Top 10 for LLM Applications** is **security guidance** — a community-maintained
list of the most critical risks specific to LLM systems. It is **not a law or standard**;
it is a widely used checklist for security reviews.

It touches the parts of the atlas where untrusted text meets action: **prompt engineering**
(the injection surface grows with the untrusted content you assemble), **tool calling**
(insecure output handling and excessive agency — the model's output becomes an action),
**RAG** (the retrieval corpus is an attack surface — poisoned documents become "grounding"),
and **failure modes** (overreliance on confident-but-wrong output). This is a **stub**; a
fuller risk-by-risk treatment is planned, and the connections shown are accurate today.
