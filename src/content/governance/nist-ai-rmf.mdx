The **NIST AI Risk Management Framework (AI RMF 1.0)** is a **voluntary** framework from
the US National Institute of Standards and Technology. It is **not law** — it binds no
one — but it is a widely used vocabulary for organizing AI risk work into four functions:
**Govern, Map, Measure, Manage**.

It maps cleanly onto the technical layer of the atlas: **Measure** is where
**evaluation** lives (you cannot manage what you do not measure), and **Map/Manage**
depend on understanding **failure modes** to know which risks are real and how to mitigate
them. This is a **stub** — a fuller treatment (function-by-function control mapping) is
planned; the connections shown are already accurate.
