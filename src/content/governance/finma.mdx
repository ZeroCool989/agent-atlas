**FINMA** — the **Swiss Financial Market Supervisory Authority** — is Switzerland's
independent supervisor of banks, insurers, securities firms, fund managers, and financial
market infrastructures. It is a **supervisor, not a legislature**: it does not enact
statutes. Instead it articulates how it applies Swiss financial-market law through
**circulars** (binding supervisory practice for supervised institutions) and **guidance**
(which communicates its supervisory expectations, including on the **governance and risk
management of AI**). Its expectations bind the institutions it supervises; they are not a
general-purpose AI law.

The mappings here are **potential relevance**, not determinations. Applicability, and how
much is expected, depend on the institution's licence, the materiality of the AI use, and
the Swiss supervisory context; a rule that matters for a systemically important bank may
be disproportionate for a small firm. On the atlas, FINMA's expectations land on the
operational concepts: **evaluation** and **verification** (independent validation and
in-line checks of models), **observability** (monitoring, operational risk and
resilience of production systems), and **failure modes** (the model risks a proportionate
risk assessment has to name).

> Not legal advice, and Swiss-specific. Confirm the current FINMA circulars and guidance,
> and how they apply to a given licence and activity, before relying on any of this.
